GARDEN PLATE

The goal of creating Garden Plate was to aid working professionals find enticing recipes and waste less while cooking at home.

contributed members: Michael Khoury, Amy Ko, Zubie Taupan, Kaicheng Yuan

THE RESEARCH:

The objective of our research was to understand what actions customers take when they consider their recipe choices, buying groceries for intended recipes and how much left-over ingredients they have after a meal. The methodology used for this study was, one-on-one generative research interviews to better understand our users and empathize with their needs. We interviewed five participants ranging from ages 24-39, who had general interests in cooking, and were the main individual purchasing groceries for their household. Participants also had a focus on “healthy” eating and discourage food waste. Interviews were recorded with Otter.ai and transcripts were referred back to when creating our user-persona.

THE PROBLEM STATEMENT:

We have observed that young working professionals are avoiding cooking at home by relying on food delivery services and wasting groceries due to lack of inspiration or desire to cook. How might we help our users repurpose the ingredients they already have while providing them with easy to cook recipes that don’t require a lot of time or skills?

  • ITERATION 1
    We noticed from the user testing is that users did not know that scrolling was an option. Our hi-fi prototype reflects that there is more on the page and that scrolling is an option.
  • ITERATION 2
    Feedback from our user testing approved of the option to use the app as a guest. This would save time for first time users to get introduced to Garden Plate without the commitment of signing up.
  • ITERATION 3
    A notable iteration we made was to indicate that there was a drop down option to watch the recipe videos.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

This is our final product after the user testings. While there is evidence of large amounts of food waste every year, since the pandemic, there hasn’t been insight to what those numbers look like now. The user interviews suggested that rather than food waste, there wasn’t even enough time to buy foods to not use. We followed the user's needs: wanting to cook from home, having little time to cook, and wanting to keep healthy food practices in mind is where Garden Plate sprouted from.
